Elephant

General Facts

  1. African elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. They are slightly larger than their Asian cousins and can be identified by their larger ears that look somewhat like the continent of Africa.
  2. Elephant ears radiate heat to help keep these large animals cool
  3. Elephants are fond of water and enjoy showering by sucking water into their trunks and spraying it all over themselves. Afterwards, they often spray their skin with a protective coating of dust.
  4. An elephant's trunk is actually a long nose used for smelling, breathing, trumpeting, drinking, and also for grabbing things—especially a potential meal. The trunk alone contains about 40,000 muscles.
  5. Both male and female African elephants have tusks, which are continuously growing teeth. They use these tusks to dig for food and water and strip bark from trees.
  6. Elephants eat roots, grasses, fruit, and bark, and they eat a lot of these things. An adult elephant can consume up to 300 pounds of food in a single day.
  7. Elephants are matriarchal, meaning they live in female-led groups. The matriarch is usually the biggest and oldest.
  8. Elephants have a longer pregnancy than any other mammal—almost 22 months.
  9. Poaching for the ivory trade is the biggest threat to African elephants’ survival.
  10. African elephants range throughout the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa and the rainforests of central and West Africa
