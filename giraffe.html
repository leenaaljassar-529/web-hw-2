Giraffe

General Facts

  1. Giraffes are the world's tallest mammals, thanks to their towering legs and long necks.
  2. Typically, these fascinating animals roam the open grasslands in small groups of about half a dozen..
  3. Giraffes use their height to good advantage and browse on leaves and buds in treetops that few other animals can reach
  4. The 21-inch tongue helps them pluck tasty morsels from branches. Giraffes eat most of the time and, like cows, regurgitate food and chew it as cud.
  5. A giraffe eats hundreds of pounds of leaves each week and must travel miles to find enough food.
  6. The giraffe's height also helps it to keep a sharp lookout for predators across the wide expanse of the African savanna.
  7. The giraffe's stature can be a disadvantage as well—it is difficult and dangerous for a giraffe to drink at a water hole.
  8. Giraffes only need to drink once every several days; they get most of their water from the luscious plants they eat.
  9. Giraffes have beautiful spotted coats. While no two individuals have exactly the same pattern, giraffes from the same area appear similar.
  10. Female giraffes give birth standing up.These infants can stand in half an hour and run with their mothers an incredible ten hours after birth.

Key Facts
