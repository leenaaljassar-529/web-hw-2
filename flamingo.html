Flamingo

General Facts

  1. When a flamingo spots potential dinner—favorite foods include shrimp, snails, and plantlike water organisms called algae—it plunges its head into the water, twists it upside down, and scoops the fish using its upper beak like a shovel.
  2. They are able to "run" on water, thanks to their webbed feet, to gain speed before lifting up into the sky.
  3. Flamingos build nests that look like mounds of mud along waterways.
  4. The parents take turns sitting on the egg to keep it warm. After about 30 days, the egg hatches.
  5. Flamingo young are born white, with soft, downy feathers and a straight bill. The bill gradually curves downward as the flamingo matures.
  6. The young leave the nest after about five days to join other young flamingos in small groups, returning to the parents for food. The parents identify their chick by its voice.
  7. Most flamingo species are not endangered, although the Andean flamingo is listed as Vulnerable, and the Chilean, Lesser, and Puna flamingos are Near Threatened.
  8. These birds live in shallow lakes, mangrove swamps and sandy islands of Africa, Asia, America and Europe.
  9. Color of their feather is result of their diet: pigments in their food (called carotenoids) are responsible for red and pink colors of their feathers. They eat shrimps, algae, crustaceans…
  10. The visible "knee" of the flamingo is actually ankle joint. True knee is close to the body and can't be seen because of the feathers.
