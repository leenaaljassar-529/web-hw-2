Lion

General Facts

  1. African lions have been admired throughout history for as symbols of courage and strength.
  2. African lions once roamed most of Africa and parts of Asia and Europe, but now they can only be found today in parts of sub-Saharan Africa.
  3. Prides are family units that may comprise anywhere from two to 40 lions—including up to to three or four males, a dozen or so females, and their young
  4. Female lions are the pride's primary hunters and leaders. They often work together to prey upon antelopes, zebras, wildebeest, and other large animals of the open grasslands.
  5. Young lions do not help to hunt until they are about a year old.
  6. The name for a baby lion is a cub, whelp or lionet.
  7. Lions scent mark their territory, using their wee, to create a border.
  8. An adult male’s roar can be heard up to 8km away.
  9. Male lions are much bigger than females and they have large manes, or hair, which covers their head and neck
  10. Lions are the laziest of all the big cats, and can sleep for 16-20 hours each day.
